About the Program

As the Binghamton University Bike Share, we know that each student has his or her biking needs. Some may not be willing to pay the hundreds needed for a new bike. Others may not have convenient storage space. Still, others may simply not ride often enough to warrant buying a bike. We want to provide a quality biking experience that is fun, safe, and easy for all by suiting individual needs.

Bicycles have several benefits. They are environmentally-friendly alternatives to cars, yet give users the flexibility to ride where they need to go, when they need to go there. Rather than depending only on restrictive bus schedules, combining bicycling with public transit (with bus-bike racks) leads to an affordable and green way of transportation. Riding bikes also helps improve health and wellness, especially around the hilly Binghamton terrain. Insufficient parking and traffic congestion may be alleviated, and a bike share provides a great opportunity and platform to reduce campus carbon footprints and increase student engagement. Lastly, riding bikes is fun, and group rides can be a great way to meet new people!

By launching our pilot program in November of 2013, BUBS has ignited the interest of students and faculty. Eventually, we would like to expand the program into the city for residents to use in conjunction with the proposed Greenway project, an integral connection between the campus and the City of Binghamton.

Press Release

The Binghamton Bike Share is a student-run program that provide bikes to students and faculty at Binghamton University. The pilot program was launched November 1, 2013 and had over 160 hours of use and 40 unique users in its first month. The program is currently shut down for the winter, but will relaunch in the spring as early as weather permits. We currently have 7 bikes, but as more funds are acquired we will hopefully obtain more bikes from Cornell’s or Stony Brook’s bike sharing programs.

In the near future, we will be constructing an automated hub system on our campus which will allow registered users to borrow bikes and ride them around the campus and community. The program aims to reduce automobile traffic on campus by allowing users to ride from the commuter lots and residence halls to other areas around campus quickly and efficiently. Eventual goals include connecting the campus and community with a pedestrian and bike trail and expanding the system to the city of Binghamton.

Program Implementation

Phase I

  • This phase has been completed. The Bike Share's pilot phase was intended to speed up the process of introducing the concept of a Bike Share to Binghamton's campus. By doing so, the student acclimated to the idea of a bike share by incorporating it into daily life. It also served as a beneficial advertisement for the program as it began to expand into further phases of development.
  • The pilot phase included 7 bicycles donated from the Outdoor Pursuits office (see Bike Unit section). They were manually signed out. The hardware was simple and straightforward to use. Ease of use of the bike share is the main priority in order to encourage frequent use by the student body.

Phase II

  • The second phase of the program is meant to expand both the number of bicycles and the main method of rental.
  • Once more funding is given to the bike share more bikes will be purchased.
  • Additionally, the Bike Share plans to expand to 3rd generation system. A Senior Design Project for the Thomas J. Watson School of Engineering and Applied Science class of 2014 is in progress. This will allow for an electronic system to check bikes in and out, increasing efficiency and ease-of-use by eliminating the need for manual check-out. Users will also be able to check current bicycle locations via the internet and/or SmartPhone application. Ideally, a designated Bike Share coordinator will be assigned a paid position each school year to ensure that the program continues.
  • Hubs will be located in accordance with where students most need them. Potential locations include Bartle Library, Lecture Hall, Union, East and West Gym, ITC Center, and the residential communities.
  • BUBS will keep up-to-date on improvements and headway made by bike share programs at other universities and cities and incorporate relevant lessons learned into the program.

Phase III

  • The third phase of the program includes the expansion to Downtown Binghamton to allow members of the community to participate. We hope that this expansion will coincide with the Binghamton Greenway Project. The Greenway, a pedestrian and bicycling-only pathway connection between BU and downtown, will make it possible for efficient and quick transportation to and from campus. As a result of the expansion, BUBS will promote a healthy and sustainable lifestyle for the campus and surrounding community.

Frequently Asked Questions 

Q: What is the Binghamton Bike Share?
A: The Binghamton Bike Share is a free service that allows any student or faculty member to borrow a bike.

Q: How much does it cost to use the bike share?
A: The bike share is a free program for all users. Fees will be charged to anyone who damages the system or does not abide by our rules.

Q: How do I register for the program?
A: In order to register for the bike share, you can sign up on our website or via Facebook. You can also register directly at the Information Desk located in the Old Union.

Q: How do I take out a bike?
A: Go to the Old Union Information Desk and you will be given a key that will unlock one of our bikes.

Q: Where can I find the bikes?
A: Our bike rack is located under the engineering building near the library. The bikes available for use have a bright green flag with a number on the flag.

Q: How long may I borrow a bike for?
A: You may borrow a Bike Share bike for up to 4 hours before returning the bike and key.

Q: What do I do if I get a flat tire while riding a bike?
A: Return the bike as soon as possible and e-mail us at BinghamtonBikeShare@gmail.com. Once you return it, feel free to borrow another bike.

Q: Do I have to wear a helmet while riding a Bike Share bike?
A: We offer helmets to each user and we strongly recommend the use for your general safety. However, NYS law does not require that helmets be worn.

Q: Can my friends use the bikes even if they don’t go to Binghamton?
A: Yes, you can sign out bikes for friends with your own ID card. However, the responsibility for the bikes is on whoever rents out the bikes, so make sure they don’t break the bikes or the rules!

Q: What happens if I do not return the bike on time?
A: You will be charged a fee of $5 per hour for the first two hours you are late. After, you will be charged $10 per hour. If you keep the bike overnight, you will be charged an additional $25.

Q: What happens if I damage the bike?
A: Any damage that you cause to a bike will be charged to you through Student Accounts based on how much maintenance will be required.

Q: When is the program open?
A: Bike Share operates daily from 12-8:00 PM. The program will be open for the spring and the fall. The program stops running when the first significant snowfall occurs. For more 
updates on the Bike Share, like our Facebook page! 

Q: When will you get more racks?
A: As soon as we renew our subscription to Playboy.

Q: The bike seat is too high/low for me. What do I do
A: Adjust the bike seat by opening the quick-release lever, raising or lowering the seat post, and reclosing the lever. We will add informative videos to our website.

Q: Can I donate my own bike to the program?
A: Yes! If you wish to donate your bike, please contact us and we can easily implement your bike into the system.

Q: Am I allowed to take the bike off campus?
A: Yes, as long as the bike is returned on time and proper locking techniques are being used. It is your responsibility to bring the bike back in working order.

Q: How do I lock my bike?
A: Attached to each bike is a lock for your convenience. There is a video on our website on how to use the lock. 

Q: I have my own bike that needs repair. Where can I get help?
A: Bike Share runs a repair shop in the basement of the Old Union, next to the Undergrounds.

Q: How do I return my bike?
A: Lock the rear tire of the bike to our bike rack in front of the Engineering Building. Return the key at the Old Union desk and sign out. Record any damage or reviews on the bike or the 
   system. If you are the last person to use the bike and there are damages, you may be charged.

Q: How can I join the maintenance team?
A: If you are interested in joining our team, Outdoor Pursuits is offering a class (CEO 395) to teach bike share members how to repair bikes. Contact us for more information.

Q: Am I allowed to ride a bike while under the influence?
A: No. It is unsafe and illegal to ride while under the influence of drugs or alcohol.

Q: How do I become part of the Binghamton Bike Share?
A: We hold our meetings every Monday! Contact us for times!

Q: How can I contact the Binghamton Bike Share?
A: You can contact us via our Facebook page or email us at BinghamtonBikeShare@gmail.com.
